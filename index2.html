A Digital Exploration in Black Expressive Life

This portfolio reflects my journey through the AADHum fellowship, where I explored the intersections of Black culture, technology, and digital humanities. It showcases a collection of projects that experiment with glitch effects, immersive visuals, and creative uses of digital tools.

The Script: All about AADHum

Explore how AADHum bridges the gap between technology and Black culture. My script is tailored for high school students and loving elders, helping them understand the initiative’s purpose and opportunities.

AADHum Tour: Main Room

Guiding visitors through the heart of AADHum, highlighting the technologies and resources that bring together art, technology, and culture in one space.

Inventory: Technological Ecosystems

A thematic collection exploring memory-preserving technologies within AADHum, showcasing how these tools help re-contextualize cultural spaces.

Textile Mapping / Physical Computing

Using embroidery and computing, this project explores contested histories, wearable narratives, and interactive textiles.

Photogrammetry & 3D Rendered Environments

Techniques for reanimating spaces, helping us revisit and reinterpret historical environments through 3D rendering.

Final Deliverables

Artifacts and projects created during the fellowship that serve as enduring representations of the skills and knowledge gained.
